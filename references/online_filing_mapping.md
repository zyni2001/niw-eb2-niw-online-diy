# USCIS Online Filing Mapping

## Organize by USCIS bucket first

For online filing, the primary structure is the USCIS upload page, not a paper-style exhibit binder.

Typical I-140 online buckets:

1. Upload Form I-140
2. Evidence Supporting National Interest Waiver
3. An official academic record
4. Proof of exceptional abilities
5. Form ETA-9089
6. Form ETA Appendix A
7. Form ETA-750/750B
8. Additional Evidence

## Recommended packet naming

Use packet names that are easy to cite in the petition letter:

- `NIW-01_Petition_Letter.pdf`
- `NIW-02_Government_Policy_And_Standards_Packet.pdf`
- `NIW-03_Core_Publications_Packet.pdf`
- `NIW-04_Citation_And_Impact_Packet.pdf`
- `NIW-05_Peer_Review_And_Awards_Packet_Hybrid.pdf`
- `NIW-06_US_Research_Continuity_Packet.pdf`
- `NIW-07_Recommendation_Letters_Packet.pdf`

Academic records are cleaner when grouped by institution or degree:

- `AcademicRecord_USC_MS_Packet.pdf`
- `AcademicRecord_USTC_BS_Packet.pdf`
- `AcademicRecord_Berkeley_Current_PhD_Transcript_Clean.pdf`

Forms should be named plainly:

- `Form_I-140_EB2_NIW_Signed.pdf`
- `ETA-9089_Main.pdf`
- `ETA-9089_Appendix_A.pdf`
- `ETA-9089_Final_Determination.pdf`

Optional clarification PDFs can be useful when the USCIS interface presents legacy or inapplicable buckets:

- `Clarification_Proof_of_Exceptional_Abilities_Not_Used.pdf`
- `Clarification_Form_ETA_750_750B_Not_Applicable.pdf`

## Example final online upload tree

This is a concrete, upload-ready structure that works well for a self-filed advanced-degree NIW case:

1. `00_Upload_Form_I140`
   - `Form_I-140_EB2_NIW_Signed.pdf`
2. `01_Evidence_Supporting_National_Interest_Waiver`
   - `NIW-01_Petition_Letter.pdf`
   - `NIW-02_Government_Policy_And_Standards_Packet.pdf`
   - `NIW-03_Core_Publications_Packet.pdf`
   - `NIW-04_Citation_And_Impact_Packet.pdf`
   - `NIW-05_Peer_Review_And_Awards_Packet_Hybrid.pdf`
   - `NIW-06_US_Research_Continuity_Packet.pdf`
   - `NIW-07_Recommendation_Letters_Packet.pdf`
3. `02_An_Official_Academic_Record`
   - `AcademicRecord_USC_MS_Packet.pdf`
   - `AcademicRecord_USTC_BS_Packet.pdf`
   - `AcademicRecord_Berkeley_Current_PhD_Transcript_Clean.pdf`
4. `03_Proof_of_Exceptional_Abilities`
   - normally left empty for advanced-degree NIW
   - optional `Clarification_Proof_of_Exceptional_Abilities_Not_Used.pdf`
5. `04_Form_ETA_9089`
   - `ETA-9089_Main.pdf`
6. `05_Form_ETA_Appendix_A`
   - `ETA-9089_Appendix_A.pdf`
7. `06_Form_ETA_750_750B`
   - normally left empty
   - optional `Clarification_Form_ETA_750_750B_Not_Applicable.pdf`
8. `07_Additional_Evidence`
   - `ETA-9089_Final_Determination.pdf`
   - `AdditionalEvidence_Official_Public_And_News_Corroboration_Packet.pdf`
   - `AdditionalEvidence_Optional_Status_Documents_Packet.pdf`

## Petition-letter citation style

Prefer packet references over old-style exhibit numbering:

- `See the Government Policy and Standards Packet.`
- `See the Core Publications Packet.`
- `See the Recommendation Letters Packet.`

This is clearer for online filing than an exhibit system copied from paper filings.

## What belongs where

### Evidence Supporting NIW

- petition letter
- government policy / standards packet
- publications packet
- citation / impact packet
- peer review / awards packet, often as a hybrid PDF if screenshot-heavy pages require flattening
- U.S. research continuity packet
- recommendation letters packet

### An official academic record

- packetized degree certificates and transcripts
- use a clean, non-encrypted transcript if the school-issued PDF is password-protected or encrypted

### Proof of exceptional abilities

Only use if the filing is truly relying on exceptional ability.
If the case is advanced-degree NIW, this bucket can stay empty or contain only a short note if needed.

### Additional Evidence

Use for:

- ETA final determination when USCIS does not provide a dedicated bucket for it
- secondary corroboration packets such as official public materials and news
- optional status documents
- other clearly secondary or explanatory materials

## File-count and size strategy

- USCIS commonly limits individual files to about 12 MB.
- Keep each packet below that when possible.
- If a corroboration packet grows too large, split it by function rather than by arbitrary page count.
